Reflection on time and habit techniques

10 January 2017



Timebox

A Timebox is a maximum amount of time which you allocate to an activity. For example, you might allocate two hours to researching a topic, and when that two hour timebox is up, you would then stop researching (no matter what your progress).

Timeboxing is common in the Scrum framework. Teams of people timebox their meetings and their work periods, to make time work for them more effectively.

I like timeboxing because I am prone to focus on details and forget the big picture. Having a timebox on an activity stops me from spending too much time tweaking the fine details when my time could be spent more valuably elsewhere.

Starting small

One tip that often crops up in time management and habit forming is starting small. In the context of time management, I've employed a variation of this technique for a number of years. Here's my version:

  • A big task, eg an essay to write, is looming.
  • Motivation is low, because the task looks so big it's hard to know where to start.
  • Time for some self-misdirection... start with the mental dialogue I'll just open it up, just to take a quick look. All I need to do is open it and have a quick look
  • Having convinced myself, I open the essay and have a look. Success!
  • Now for some more misdirection... While I'm here, I may as well just tidy up a couple of things... I'll just a do a couple more quick things.
  • 1 hour later, still going! They say the hardest part is building up the momentum to start. I like to trick myself into starting, by just doing a very minimal first step, and allowing the momentum to develop.
